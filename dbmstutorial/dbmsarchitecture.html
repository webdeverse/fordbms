DBMS Architecture



The Three Levels of the Architecture



The DBMS architecture is divided into three levels, known as the internal, conceptual, external level.

  • The internal level refers to the physical storage. It is the one concerned with how the data is physically stored.
  • The conceptual level is a "mediator" or a "level of indirection" between the internal level and external level.
  • The external level si the user interaction level. It is the one concerned with how data is viewed by individual users.
